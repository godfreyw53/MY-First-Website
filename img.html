Nairobi, the "Green City in the Sun," began as a British railway depot in 1899, named after the Maasai phrase Enkare Nairobi for "place of cool waters". It grew from a swamp into the capital and largest city of Kenya, blending modernity with vibrant street life, the rich aroma of grilled nyama choma, and the constant pulse of its diverse population navigating economic challenges and daily life.
