Cities in Kenya

  • Nakuru
  • Nairobi
  • Kakamega

Nakuru's history began with ancient human settlements, evidenced by archaeological finds at Hyrax Hill, and was formalized as a township in 1904 by the British during the colonial era. It grew from a township to a municipality in 1952, becoming a commercial and political hub in the White Highlands and a significant area for agriculture. Post-independence, it served as a home to Kenya's presidents and prominent politicians before its official charter as a city in 2021, making it Kenya's fourth-largest urban center

Kakamega's history began as an indigenous meeting point before becoming a British administrative post in 1903, eventually replacing the older trading center of Mumias after gold was discovered in the area in 1931, triggering a "gold rush" and rapid growth for the town. Its name is derived from the local practice of "pinching" ugali (corn meal), a practice that the early European settlers observed and emulated.
